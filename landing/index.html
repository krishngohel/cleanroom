The Problem

Why teams like yours
can't use public AI

Sending sensitive data to OpenAI, Anthropic, or Google creates real legal, compliance, and competitive risk. These aren't theoretical concerns.

Your data trains their models

Most commercial AI providers use your prompts and data to improve their models. Patient records, contracts, and financials sent to the cloud become training data you can't recall.

Compliance becomes a liability

HIPAA, SOC 2, PCI DSS, and GDPR all have strict rules about data leaving your control. Every cloud AI call is a potential audit finding — or a breach notification.

No visibility into what's happening

Who on your team queried what? When? With which data? Cloud AI gives you zero audit trail for access, prompts, or outputs — a nightmare for regulated industries.

Setup Process

Up and running in minutes

Cleanroom AI ships as a Docker Compose stack. One command gives your entire team a fully private AI platform.

1

Deploy the Stack

Run make dev to start FastAPI, PostgreSQL, Redis, and Ollama via Docker Compose. Works on any Linux server, Mac, or cloud VM.

2

Connect Your Data

Add SQL or filesystem connectors through the admin panel. Cleanroom queries your data in-place — nothing is ever copied to an external service.

3

Chat, Build, Automate

Your team chats with private AI, admins build workflows, and developers plug into the OpenAI-compatible API to build internal tools — all on your network.
